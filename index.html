Our Latest Notable People List

  • Greta Thunberg

    Greta Thunberg is a Swedish environmental activist known for her outspoken advocacy on climate change. Starting with school strikes in front of the Swedish parliament, her movement grew into a global phenomenon, inspiring millions of young people to demand urgent climate action. She has addressed world leaders at the United Nations and received numerous awards for her efforts.

  • Elon Musk

    Elon Musk is an entrepreneur and engineer, known for his roles as the CEO of SpaceX and Tesla. He is a visionary leader in the fields of space exploration, electric vehicles, and sustainable energy. Musk's ambitious projects include the colonization of Mars, the development of the Hyperloop, and the advancement of AI technology. He is often cited as one of the most influential figures in technology.

  • Kamala Harris

    Kamala Harris made history in 2021 as the first female Vice President of the United States, as well as the first African American and Asian American to hold the office. Before her vice presidency, she served as a U.S. Senator from California and the state's Attorney General. Harris is known for her work on civil rights, criminal justice reform, and immigration.

  • Malala Yousafzai

    Malala Yousafzai is a Pakistani activist for female education and the youngest-ever Nobel Prize laureate. She rose to prominence after surviving a Taliban assassination attempt in 2012, which was a response to her activism for girls' education. Malala has since become a global symbol of the fight for girls' rights to education and has co-authored the book "I Am Malala."

  • Tim Cook

    Tim Cook is the CEO of Apple Inc., where he has overseen significant growth and innovation since taking over from Steve Jobs in 2011. Under his leadership, Apple has introduced new products such as the Apple Watch and AirPods, and he has guided the company towards a greater focus on privacy, renewable energy, and social responsibility.

  • Amanda Gorman

    Amanda Gorman is an American poet and activist who gained international acclaim after reciting her poem "The Hill We Climb" at the 2021 U.S. Presidential Inauguration. Gorman's work focuses on issues of oppression, feminism, race, and marginalization, as well as the African diaspora. She is the youngest inaugural poet in U.S. history and has since signed major publishing deals.

  • Dr. Anthony Fauci

    Dr. Anthony Fauci is an American physician and immunologist who has served as the Director of the National Institute of Allergy and Infectious Diseases since 1984. He became a household name during the COVID-19 pandemic, providing critical guidance and information to the public and government. Fauci is widely respected for his work in public health and infectious diseases.

  • Naomi Osaka

    Naomi Osaka is a Japanese professional tennis player who has won multiple Grand Slam titles. She is known for her powerful playing style and has been ranked No. 1 by the Women's Tennis Association. Beyond her athletic achievements, Osaka has been an outspoken advocate for mental health, racial justice, and gender equality, using her platform to raise awareness on these issues.

  • Jeff Bezos

    Jeff Bezos is the founder of Amazon, the world's largest online retailer, and Blue Origin, a private spaceflight company. Under his leadership, Amazon revolutionized e-commerce, cloud computing, and digital streaming. In 2021, Bezos stepped down as Amazon's CEO to focus on his other ventures, including space exploration and philanthropy.

  • Billie Eilish

    Billie Eilish is a Grammy-winning singer and songwriter known for her unique voice and style. She first gained attention with her debut single "Ocean Eyes" and has since become one of the most influential artists of her generation. Eilish is also an advocate for mental health awareness and environmental causes, often using her music and public appearances to promote these issues.

  • Sundar Pichai

    Sundar Pichai is the CEO of Alphabet Inc. and its subsidiary Google LLC. Pichai has overseen major developments at Google, including the growth of Android, Chrome, and Google Cloud. Known for his leadership and vision, Pichai has guided the company through technological advancements while advocating for ethical AI development and digital privacy.

  • Jacinda Ardern

    Jacinda Ardern is the Prime Minister of New Zealand, known for her progressive policies and compassionate leadership. She has been praised globally for her handling of the COVID-19 pandemic, as well as her response to the Christchurch mosque shootings. Ardern's focus on social justice, climate change, and inclusivity has made her a role model for leaders worldwide.

  • Xi Jinping

    Xi Jinping is the President of the People's Republic of China and the General Secretary of the Chinese Communist Party. As China's paramount leader, Xi has consolidated power and led the country through significant economic and geopolitical shifts. His policies and leadership style have had a profound impact on China's role in global affairs.

  • Taylor Swift

    Taylor Swift is an American singer-songwriter and one of the best-selling music artists of all time. She is known for her narrative songwriting and versatility across multiple genres. Swift has won numerous awards, including Grammys, and is an advocate for artists' rights, education, and disaster relief. She continues to influence the music industry and pop culture worldwide.

  • Simone Biles

    Simone Biles is an American gymnast and one of the most decorated gymnasts of all time. Known for her powerful and complex routines, Biles has set new standards in gymnastics. She has also become a prominent advocate for mental health and athletes' well-being, openly discussing her own struggles during the 2020 Tokyo Olympics.
